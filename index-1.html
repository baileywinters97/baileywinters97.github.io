Marion McClellan

A January Dandelion

By George Marion McClellan | 1860

All Nashville is a chill. And everywhere

Like desert sand, when the winds blow,

There is each moment sifted through the air,

A powdered blast of January snow.

O! thoughtless Dandelion, to be misled

By a few warm days to leave thy natural bed,

Was folly growth and blooming over soon.

And yet, thou blasted yellow-coated gem,

Full many a heart has but a common boon

With thee, now freezing on thy slender stem.

When the heart has bloomed by the touch of love's warm breath

Then left and chilling snow is sifted in,

It still may beat but there is blast and death

To all that blooming life that might have been.

George Marion McClellan was a minister, teacher, writer, and poet.

He obtained his degree from Fisk University in Nashville, Tennessee.

He had later married Augusta Rabb in 1888 in Nashville, Tennessee.

His poetry has a special language. It speaks of religion, nature,

and romance, as well as showing the true face of discrimination.

One of his poems, "To Theodore", was written about his son, who sadly

died from complications of tuberculosis. George continued to fight

for racial justice throughout his entire life. He then died on May 17th,1934

at the age of 73.
